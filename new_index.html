Bowie History Atlas

& Military History Visualizations

The author is a lifelong resident of the City of Bowie, recently retired after a career in federal service, and now with time to pursue long-standing interests in military and local history. The Bowie History Atlas is a source-linked record of the town's colonial, equestrian, and civic story — written by someone who grew up here and lived through much of it. The military history visualizations were built as personal study aids, designed to allow quick navigation between related hardware, campaigns, and primary source links across a broad span of American military history. Research by the author; development and coding by Claude AI (Anthropic).

Bowie History Atlas — six chapters
Chapter 1 · 1681–1864
Colonial Origins
Belair Mansion, the Ogle governors, slavery and resistance at the plantation, and Rochambeau’s column passing on the Yorktown campaign
Chapter 2 · 1853–1914
Railroad Era
The Baltimore & Potomac Railroad, the junction town of Huntington, how Bowie got its name, and the Garfield assassination connection
Chapter 3 · 1752–1985
Racing & Horses
Selima and the founding of American thoroughbred racing, 71 years at Bowie Race Track, Belair Stud’s Triple Crown dynasty, and the track’s strange chronicles
Chapter 4 · 1917–1957
The Woodwards
William Woodward’s racing stable, Nashua’s 1955 triumph and the shooting that ended the era, Deliberate Cruelty, and the Belair estate sale to Levitt
Chapter 5 · 1957–present
Modern Bowie
Levitt’s postwar suburb, forced busing and its consequences, Bowie State University, City Hall’s three buildings, the police department, and the schools
Chapter 6 · ongoing
People & Places
Eva Cassidy, Paul Reed Smith, Ricky Arnold, JC Chasez, Six Flags America across five names, Belt Woods old-growth forest, and a Bowie neighbor remembered
