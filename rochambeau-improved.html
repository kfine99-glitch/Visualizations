Washington–Rochambeau March to Yorktown · 1781 (IMPROVED)

Enhanced interactive swim-lane timeline of the 680-mile Franco-American march from Providence, Rhode Island, to Yorktown, Virginia (June–October 1781). Six parallel tracks show the main allied army, Lauzun’s Legion (cavalry advance guard), Washington’s personal party, the French infantry water route via Annapolis, the artillery overland via Georgetown, and Admiral de Grasse’s fleet converging from the Caribbean.

New in this version: Clickable lane legend (filters/hides lanes), search bar, “Focus Bowie MD” button (spotlights the artillery route through your area), smoother node hover/glow, improved detail panel, fixed note consistency, and better mobile touch support.

Solid nodes = primary-source confirmed camps. Dashed = inferred. Gold ring = key event. Click any node for details + map zoom. Drag timeline to pan.

Primary sources: Rochambeau Map Collection (Library of Congress) · NPS Washington-Rochambeau Revolutionary Route · W3R Association · Berthier & Closen journals · Robert Selig / Reyes Maryland surveys · Bowie Living historic blog.

About This Enhanced Visualization

In the summer and fall of 1781, approximately 17,000 Franco-American troops converged on Yorktown in one of history’s most complex joint operations. This improved version traces all six parallel elements of the 680-mile march with new interactive features for easier exploration.

Highlights added: Lane filters, search, Bowie-specific spotlight (John Easton’s Plantation, Sacred Heart Church, Belair Mansion — all on the artillery/wagon-train route through modern Bowie, MD), and polished UI.

Solid borders = confirmed by primary sources. Dashed = inferred. Gold rings = key narrative moments.

How to Use (New Features)

Drag timeline to pan. Click nodes for details + map zoom. Click legend dots to toggle lanes on/off. Use the search bar to jump to any camp. Click the new floating buttons for instant navigation. Escape closes panels.
