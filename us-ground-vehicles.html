Connection Line Guide

Lineage — Direct evolution. One vehicle was formally developed from its predecessor; the design team built on the earlier platform's architecture, chassis, or powerplant. Example: M46 → M47 → M48 → M60 Patton series.
Shared Hull / Platform — The same chassis or hull was repurposed for a different role, usually crossing into another lane. Example: M4 Sherman hull → M10 Wolverine tank destroyer, M32 TRV, and M7 Priest howitzer. The Sparrows Point armor plate connects all of them.
Influence — One design inspired or shaped another without being a direct parent. Shared concepts, doctrine, or components — but not shared hardware. Example: M4 Sherman → M26 Pershing; the Sherman's limitations drove the Pershing's design requirements.
