Washington–Rochambeau March to Yorktown · 1781

An interactive swim-lane timeline of the 680-mile Franco-American march from Providence, Rhode Island, to Yorktown, Virginia (June–October 1781). Six parallel tracks show the main allied army, Lauzun's Legion (cavalry advance guard), Washington's personal party, the French infantry water route via Annapolis, the artillery overland via Georgetown, and Admiral de Grasse's fleet converging from the Caribbean.

Solid nodes indicate well-documented camps from primary sources (Berthier journals, Rochambeau map collection at the Library of Congress). Dashed borders indicate inferred or poorly-documented camps. Gold-ringed nodes mark key narrative events. Click any node to open its detail panel and zoom the map to that location. Drag the timeline to pan; the right panel shows a live map.

About This Visualization

In the summer and fall of 1781, approximately 17,000 Franco-American troops converged on Yorktown, Virginia, in one of history’s most complex joint military operations. This visualization traces six parallel elements of the 680-mile march from Providence, Rhode Island: the main allied army under Washington and Rochambeau; Lauzun’s cavalry riding 10–15 miles ahead as an advance guard; Admiral de Grasse’s fleet converging from the Caribbean; Washington’s personal party riding ahead to Mount Vernon; the French infantry boarding ships at Annapolis; and the artillery and baggage train crossing the Potomac overland at Georgetown. The campaign ended with Cornwallis’s surrender on October 19, 1781.

Solid node borders indicate well-documented camps confirmed by primary sources. Dashed borders mark inferred or poorly-documented locations, particularly the artillery route through Maryland. Gold-ringed nodes mark key narrative turning points.

A note on the map lines: The colored polylines connecting camp markers show the general sequence and direction of travel — they are not renderings of actual roads marched. Eighteenth-century armies followed colonial post roads and turnpikes, crossed rivers at established ferry points, and frequently diverged from straight-line paths to navigate terrain, secure water, and manage logistics. The artillery and wagon train in particular made a sweeping northern detour to ford the Susquehanna at Bald Friar before rejoining the main route. Camp locations are georeferenced as precisely as available documentation permits; treat the connecting lines as schematic, not cartographic.

How to Use

Drag the timeline left or right to pan through the four-month campaign. Click any camp node to open a detail panel with dates, historical notes, and a primary source link; the map on the right zooms to the selected location. The six color-coded swim lanes run top to bottom: De Grasse’s fleet (blue), Lauzun’s Legion (amber), Washington’s party (gold), main allied army (teal), infantry water route (purple), and artillery overland (red). Click the title bar to open the full About description. Press Escape to close panels.
